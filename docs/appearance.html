Themes & appearance

Ten live themes, a Dark-mode toggle, TrueType fonts, and real resolution choice, all from the Control Panel and all without a reboot.

The Control Panel

The Control Panel is where you change how UnoDOS looks: a Theme menu, a Dark mode toggle, a Resolution menu, a system-wide Font picker, a UI scale menu (100–200%), a Volume slider (it adjusts the sound output live, even mid-note, on machines with HD Audio or AC'97 audio), and the clock: the time is set with two spinners, and the date with the Set date… calendar picker — click a day and it is applied. It opens on first boot and from the Start menu.

The ten themes

Aurora is the modern default, with soft shadows, rounded windows, a frosted taskbar and a coloured underline under the active window title. The other eight are faithful retro looks. Choosing a theme instantly re-skins the whole desktop.

TrueType fonts

All the on-screen text is drawn by a TrueType engine with proper proportional spacing and kerning. Four faces are included: Chicago (the crisp bitmap-style default), Sans, Mono and Ubuntu. Pick one and everything restyles — titles, labels, buttons, lists — and the whole layout re-measures itself to fit the new face.

UI scale

The UI scale menu makes everything bigger without changing the resolution: 100%, 125%, 150% or 200%. Every font scales and every window, menu and toolbar re-lays itself out to match — handy on high-resolution laptop panels.

Resolution & scaling

Pick a resolution from the Control Panel and the desktop resizes to match, scaled to fill your screen while keeping the correct proportions.
